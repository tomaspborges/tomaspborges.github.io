I am a PhD candidate in Political Science at IESP-UERJ, currently a visiting PhD student at MIT (advisor: Daniel F. Hidalgo). My advisor in Brazil is Fernando Meireles. I hold fellowships from FAPERJ Nota 10 and CAPES PDSE.

I am a researcher at CERES and coordinator of MAPE at IESP-UERJ. I hold a Bachelor's in Applied Foreign Languages for International Negotiations from CEFET-RJ, with an academic exchange at Sciences Po Strasbourg (France). I am also a Data Scientist at Quaest.

My research focuses on distributive politics, electoral behavior, and social protection — with particular interest in how public goods shape voting behavior and how states build and dismantle social policies.

MAPE — Laboratório de Monitoramento e Avaliação de Políticas e Eleições

Selected Research Projects

Housing, Sanitation, and Electoral Behavior

Distributive Politics · Electoral Behavior

How do durable public goods — housing and sanitation — shape vote choice in Brazilian municipalities? This project investigates the causal link between targeted infrastructure delivery and electoral behavior.

Vote Share Forecasting at the Municipal Level

Electoral Behavior · Machine Learning

How well can we predict party vote shares across Brazilian municipalities? Combining machine learning with socioeconomic and historical electoral data to build fine-grained local-level forecasts.

Social Protection Retrenchment and Resilience

Social Protection · Comparative Politics

A comparative study of how Payment for Environmental Services (PES) programs in Brazil were dismantled during austerity periods — and how some managed to survive. Presented at ICPP7 (Montréal, 2025).

Armed Groups and Educational Inequality

Violence · Inequality · Rio de Janeiro

How do militias and drug trafficking constrain educational opportunities and reproduce inequality in the Rio de Janeiro metropolitan area? Funded by UNICEF and Open Society Foundations.

Workshops & Short Courses

Escrita Acadêmica e Publicação de Papers Quantitativos em Ciências Sociais

With Rogério Barbosa et al.  ·  IESP-UERJ  ·  2024 & 2025

Workshop aimed at equipping social science researchers to plan, write, and publish competitive quantitative papers in Brazil and internationally. Topics include: paper anatomy; writing clear abstracts and introductions with a research question, gap, and contribution; translating findings into arguments with transparent communication of effects and uncertainty; robustness and sensitivity tests; navigating the editorial system; identifying suitable journals; and strategies for international insertion. Teaching materials are available in an open repository for broad reuse.

Introdução à Análise Bibliométrica com Web of Science e VOSviewer

With Carlos Barreto Freitas  ·  IESP-UERJ  ·  2024 & 2025  ·  Extension course

Short course introducing researchers to bibliometric analysis and systematic literature mapping. Covers database searches in Web of Science, citation analysis, and network visualization with VOSviewer.
